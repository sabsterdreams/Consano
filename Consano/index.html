Bullying Between Girls in High School: Emotional Abuse Awareness

Bullying between girls in high school often goes unnoticed because it’s covert and manipulative. It’s not just “mean girl drama”—it’s emotional abuse that can deeply affect mental health. Signs include gossip, exclusion, passive-aggressive behavior, and toxic friendships.

According to BeeWell data, girls report lower wellbeing than boys, partly due to these hidden forms of bullying. Our mission is to raise awareness and provide tools for healing and resilience.

Our Mission

To validate the unseen emotional pain young girls experience in friendships and provide tools for healing, self-protection, and long-term emotional growth.

Who We Help

  • Girls experiencing friendship problems that feel like bullying or emotional abuse
  • Girls wanting to learn signs of unhealthy behavior in relationships
  • Parents, adults, and teachers seeking educational resources and safe spaces for girls

Understanding Covert Bullying

Covert bullying is less about investigating incidents and more about helping victims heal. Our resources build strength, confidence, and resilience to prevent future harm.

While not a substitute for therapy, these tools provide validation and support for girls who feel unseen.
